Why Technically Demanding

Why Electronics Thermoforming Packaging Is Technically Demanding

ESD Damage — 10 Volts Can Permanently Damage an Electronic Component
Electronic components sustain permanent ESD damage at voltages as low as 10–30 volts — levels imperceptible to human touch but permanently damaging to CMOS, MOSFET, and BiCMOS semiconductor devices. Standard thermoforming PP is an insulator (surface resistivity >10¹² Ω/sq) that generates and holds electrostatic charge. ESD-safe PP and PETG with controlled surface resistivity (10⁶–10⁹ Ω/sq) dissipates charge continuously, preventing accumulation to damaging levels.
Connector and Terminal Geometry — Precision Cavity for Pin Arrays
Electronic connectors and terminal arrays have precise pin geometries that must be protected from any mechanical deformation during transit. A bent terminal pin or contaminated connector face is an immediate assembly failure at the electronics manufacturing stage. ESD-safe thermoformed trays with precision connector protection cavities — each connector body in its own cavity with clearance for the pin array — are the industry standard.
PCB Gold Finger and Component Height Clearance
PCBs have two critical packaging dimensions: gold finger edge connectors (exposed contact pads at the PCB edge that must not be touched by packaging material) and component height (the tallest component on the assembled PCB determines the minimum cavity height). Both must be confirmed from the PCB specification before cavity design.
Static-Generating Transit Environment — Plastic Friction Charge Accumulation
Electronics components moving in packaging during transit generate electrostatic charge through plastic-to-plastic friction contact. Standard packaging materials have no means of dissipating this charge — it accumulates until it discharges through the enclosed component. ESD-safe packaging dissipates charge continuously — the accumulation mechanism is neutralised.
Pain Points We Solve

Three Problems Electronics Packaging Buyers Face

Latent ESD Damage — Field Failures Traced Back to Packaging
Problem: Electronic components passing all in-process and final tests but sustaining latent ESD damage at the packaging stage cause field failures in service — the most expensive and reputationally damaging failure mode in electronics. Root cause analysis eventually traces the damage to non-ESD-safe packaging in the supply chain. Solution: ESD-safe PP and PETG packaging with controlled surface resistivity CoA per batch — documented ESD protection traceability for the full supply chain.
Terminal and Connector Damage During Transit
Problem: Electronic connectors and terminal arrays with bent or contaminated pins cause immediate assembly failures — the connector cannot be used, a rework or scrap event results. Solution: Precision ESD-safe thermoformed cavities designed from the specific connector body and pin array geometry — each connector in its own ESD-safe cavity with pin array clearance.
Non-ESD-Safe Packaging from Thermoforming Suppliers Without ESD Capability
Problem: Standard thermoforming suppliers without ESD material capability cannot supply ESD-safe electronics packaging — but may supply standard PP without disclosing the ESD risk. Solution: ESD-safe PP and PETG thermoforming capability in-house at Vapi, with surface resistivity testing and CoA per batch. The ESD-safe specification is documented, not claimed.
