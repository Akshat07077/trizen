Pain Points We Solve

Three Problems Retail Display Packaging for Toys Buyers Face

Toy Display Orientation — Wrong Facing at Retail Shelf
Problem: Toy retail packaging where the toy is not in the correct facing orientation for the shelf display position — the toy's primary feature or character face is not visible at the consumer's sightline. Solution: Display orientation confirmed from the specific retail shelf position (eye-level facing, hanging peg orientation) before tray design.
Toy Retail Shelf Presence — Competing for Consumer Attention
Problem: Toy retail packaging with low visual impact fails to compete for the child's attention at a busy toy retail shelf where dozens of competing toy brands display simultaneously. Solution: APET crystal-clear presenting the toy's hero design at maximum visibility, combined with precision display orientation.
Counter Display Stability — Toy Tray Tips Under Product Load
Problem: Counter display toy trays that tip or are unstable under the combined weight of multiple toys damage the retail display quality. Solution: Counter display tray base dimensions and structural geometry confirmed from the toy weight and counter display unit specification.

Retail Display Packaging for Toys — Key Design Requirements

Retail Shelf Orientation — Display Position Specific
Toy display orientation confirmed from the specific retail display position — hanging peg sightline, shelf facing height, counter display angle. The toy's primary feature faces the consumer at the correct viewing angle.
APET Crystal-Clear — Maximum Toy Visibility at Retail

Counter Display Geometry — Stable Under Full Product Load

Peg Display Compatibility — Hanging Format Requirements
Hanging toy blister packs designed with hanging hole geometry matching the toy retail peg system specification. Hole position, diameter, and blister weight distribution confirmed for the standard peg system.
